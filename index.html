What is Recycling?

Recycling is a process that consists of collecting and transforming waste or waste materials into new materials or products for reuse. Recycling has many environmental and economic benefits, such as preventing the disuse of potentially useful materials, reducing the consumption of new raw materials and energy use, and decreasing pollution and garbage generation.

Why should I do it?

Recycling is a process that consists of converting waste into new products or raw materials for later use. Recycling is done for several reasons, such as:

  • Save natural resources and energy.
  • Reduce pollution and the greenhouse effect.
  • Extend the life of materials and create new products.
  • Avoid deforestation and preserve the environment.
  • Create jobs and promote the circular economy.

World impact (Importance)

If recycling is done, society and the environment are improved in many aspects. Some of the benefits of recycling in the long term are:

  • Reduce the amount of garbage and avoid the growth of landfills.
  • Conserve natural resources and protect biodiversity.
  • Save energy and money in the production of new products.
  • Reduce greenhouse gas emissions and global warming.
  • Creating new sources of employment and fostering the circular economy.
